# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard – Employee Attrition Analysis

A comprehensive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze employee attrition patterns and workforce trends within an organization. The dashboard provides actionable insights into employee turnover by age, salary, job role, education background, gender, and tenure, helping HR teams make data-driven retention decisions.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

The HR Analytics Dashboard is an interactive Power BI report that enables HR professionals and business leaders to understand the key factors driving employee attrition. By visualizing workforce demographics, compensation trends, and employee behavior, the dashboard helps identify high-risk employee segments and supports strategic workforce planning.

---

## 🛠 Tech Stack

The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:

- 📊 **Power BI Desktop** – Main data visualization platform used for report creation.
- 📂 **Power Query** – Data transformation and data cleaning.
- 🧠 **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** – Used for calculated measures, KPIs, and business logic.
- 🔗 **Data Modeling** – Relationships established between tables to enable cross-filtering and aggregation.
- 📈 **Interactive Visualizations** – KPI Cards, Donut Charts, Bar Charts, Line Charts, Treemaps, and Matrix Tables.
- 📁 **File Format** – `.pbix` for development and `.png` for dashboard previews.

---

## 📂 Data Source

**Dataset:**  HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset

The dataset contains employee-related information including:

- Employee demographics
- Department and job role details
- Salary and compensation information
- Educational background
- Years at company
- Employee attrition status
- Job satisfaction metrics

This dataset is commonly used for workforce analytics, employee retention studies, and predictive HR modeling.

# ✨ Features & Highlights

## 🔍 Business Problem

Employee attrition is one of the biggest challenges organizations face today. High employee turnover leads to increased recruitment costs, reduced productivity, loss of organizational knowledge, and decreased employee morale.

Key business questions addressed by this dashboard:

- Which employee groups are most likely to leave?
- Does salary influence employee attrition?
- Which job roles experience the highest turnover?
- How does employee tenure impact retention?
- Are specific age groups leaving more frequently?
- Which educational backgrounds contribute most to attrition?

---

## 🎯 Goal of the Dashboard

The primary goals of this dashboard are:

- Monitor workforce health and attrition trends.
- Identify high-risk employee segments.
- Analyze attrition patterns across demographics and job roles.
- Support HR decision-making through data-driven insights.
- Improve employee retention and workforce planning.

# 📈 Key Business Insights

### 🔴 Attrition Rate is High

The organization experiences a **16.1% attrition rate**, meaning approximately one out of every six employees leaves the company.

---

### 🔴 Compensation is a Major Driver

Nearly **69% of attrition** comes from employees earning less than **5K**, indicating salary plays a significant role in employee retention.

---

### 🔴 Mid-Career Employees Leave Most Frequently

Employees aged **26–35** contribute almost **49% of total attrition**, making them the highest-risk age group.

---

### 🔴 Attrition is Concentrated in Specific Roles

The following roles account for the majority of employee exits:

- Laboratory Technician
- Sales Executive
- Research Scientist
- Sales Representative

Together, they contribute approximately **84% of total attrition.**

---

### 🔴 Early Employee Retention Needs Improvement

Most employee departures occur within the first few years of employment, suggesting onboarding and engagement challenges.

---

### 🔴 Educational Background Matters

Employees with **Life Sciences** and **Medical** educational backgrounds contribute the highest levels of employee turnover.

---

# 🚀 Business Recommendations

### 1️⃣ Review Compensation Structure

- Increase pay competitiveness for lower salary brackets.
- Introduce performance-based incentives and bonuses.

### 2️⃣ Strengthen Early Employee Engagement

- Improve onboarding programs.
- Implement mentoring initiatives for new hires.

### 3️⃣ Focus on High-Risk Age Groups

- Develop career progression pathways for employees aged 26–35.
- Offer leadership development opportunities.

### 4️⃣ Retain Critical Job Roles

- Conduct role-specific retention analysis.
- Introduce targeted retention strategies for Laboratory Technicians and Sales teams.

### 5️⃣ Improve Career Growth Opportunities

- Create transparent promotion frameworks.
- Provide continuous learning and development programs.
